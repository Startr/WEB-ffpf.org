Our History



Discover the key events that have shaped our history

For over 30 years, the Foundation has worked with disadvantaged communities in India to help break the cycle of poverty. Discover how we have made a difference over the years.

THE BEGINNING

Our journey began with the initiative of Professor Dwivedi who was from the village of Barhiya, Azamgargh in the state of Uttar Pradesh, India. He was a man dedicated to his community. When his children grew up and had started to work, he decided to donate his entire salary to his community. In 1984, he helped to finance and build a school in his village. In 1987, the Foundation for Freedom from Poverty was founded.

PROJECT AFTER PROJECT

From 1988 to 2008, we built a school that provides primary and secondary education from which over 15,000 students have since graduated. Education is at the heart of all our missions as it is the most effective way to fight poverty. The Foundation also introduced technology into its programs making computers available to the students. Mobile clinics were also created and have treated over 60,000 patients. Many medicals missions have taken place including cataract surgery in partnership with the Eye Foundation of America.

THE SUCCESSION

Sadly in 2008, Professor Dwivedi, the Foundation’s founder passed away. At this time, his son, Dr. Dwivedi decided to continue his father’s impressive project of “breaking the cycle of poverty” of the most disadvantaged. As Dr. Dwivedi had immigrated to Canada, there was no longer a physical presence in the village of Barhiya to continue the Foundation’s work. In 2009, a partnership between the Freedom from Poverty Foundation and the Comprehensive Rural Health Project was created. The Foundation then moved its activities to Jamkhed, a disadvantaged community in the state of Maharashtra, India.

DEDICATION

Over the years, the Foundation has operated thanks to the generosity and skills of its volunteers. They provide freely a variety of skills and services that have allowed us to raise funds, provide equipment and expertise, all with one goal in mind: educating and improving the quality of life of the less fortunate. The Freedom from poverty foundation treated over 250,000 patients till now.



Our Vision



We aim to increase our activities, year after year, with the objective of increasing our positive impact on the community of Jamkhed. With thanks to the exceptional efforts of the volunteers we recruit from around the world, our goal is to provide the community with the tools necessary so that it has the capacity to break the cycle of poverty itself.



Our Values




DIGNITY

PASSION

EQUALITY

Partnership



Since 2009, the Freedom From Poverty Foundation has been working in partnership with the CHRP (Comprehensive Rural Health Project) in Jamkhed, a foundation that has been helping the most disadvantaged for over 45 years. This partnership ensures a permanent physical presence in the community of Jamkhed for the continuity of care and easier follow-up for the community. Both organisations share the mission of breaking the cycle of poverty by providing access to health care and fostering independence.
